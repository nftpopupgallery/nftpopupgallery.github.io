NFTs IRL

At the liminal space of digital and physical, this gallery event is an opportunity to reflect on the transiency of media.

02

Central Location

The show will be hosted at a gallery on the island on Manhattan. The show will leverage the rich arts culture of NYC to elevate NFT art on a broader stage.

03

Direct Access

Each piece will be displayed with on a high definition digital display and accompanied by a QR code to allow viewers to purchase art on-site.

Curatorial Team

Maria Hwang

Professor Hwang teaches Computer Science at the Fashion Institute of Technology. In her courses, she explores the intersection of machine learning and modern fashion design and supply chains. Her research focuses on education game development for medical patients to improve adherence to treatment plans. Her work has been supported by the National Science Foundation and the Office of Women's Health.

Professor of Computer Science, Fashion Institute of Technology

Mark Santolucito

Professor Santolucito teaching Computer Science at Barnard College, Columbia University. His courses cover techniques and history of digital art, with a focus on digital audio. He completed his PhD in Computer Science at Yale University. His work has been supported by the National Science Foundation and industry partners.

Professor of Computer Science, Barnard College

Dao Chantes

Dao is a designer and researcher specializing in product design and user research with a background in HCI, media, and communication. Her work has been featured in products and exhibitions in Montreal, Bangkok, New Haven, and the United Nations.

Independent Designer

Applications are accepted on a rolling basis. The pop-up gallery will open for one weekend in early May - free admission. All digital materials must be prepared by the artists themselves. A wall-space fee is required for all artists participating in the show - see application form for details. This fee will help pay for the space and show setup.
